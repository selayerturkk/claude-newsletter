Anthropic Product News

Claude Stays Ad-Free, Expands Platform Features

Anthropic made a clear choice this week: Claude will remain ad-free. The company explained why advertising incentives are incompatible with a genuinely helpful AI assistant, and how they plan to expand access without compromising user trust. This stance contrasts sharply with OpenAI's new self-serve ad platform inside ChatGPT, which signals a push toward commercialization with tighter API pricing tiers and new enterprise SLAs.

On the development front, Claude Platform launched self-hosted sandboxes in public beta, allowing organizations to keep sensitive files and services within their own infrastructure while agent orchestration stays on Anthropic's servers. The platform also expanded Claude Managed Agents with new capabilities including dreaming, multiagent orchestration, outcomes, and webhooks.

Anthropic expanded Project Glasswing with Claude Security in public beta and committed to scanning any open-source packages they adopt, supporting maintainers through Claude for Open Source. The timing aligns with growing concern about AI-accelerated vulnerability discovery—the company noted that models as capable as Mythos Preview will soon be developed by many AI companies, and no company has yet developed sufficient safeguards to prevent misuse.

Feature Deep-Dive

Understanding Claude's Dreaming Feature

Claude Code's new "dreaming" feature represents a fundamental shift in how coding agents learn and improve. Agents write notes to themselves about specific tasks, and when another agent works on the same code later, it can use these notes to get up to speed faster and learn from previous errors.

The dreaming system consolidates information from all these notes, spotting patterns and common issues across different tasks. In theory, this should help Claude Code learn about a particular codebase and get better at working on it over time. Think of it as creating institutional memory for your development workflow.

How to use it: Dreaming works automatically in Claude CodeAnthropic's AI coding assistant that can write, debug, and refactor code across multiple files—no setup required. As you work on projects, agents automatically capture insights about your codebase architecture, common debugging patterns, and successful approaches. Future coding sessions benefit from this accumulated knowledge, leading to more contextually appropriate suggestions and faster problem-solving.

Why it matters: This addresses a core limitation of stateless AI interactions. Instead of every coding session starting from scratch, your AI assistant develops familiarity with your specific codebase, team patterns, and project constraints. For teams shipping frequently, this could significantly reduce the time spent bringing AI tools up to speed on each task.

Broader Tech News

Cursor Composer 2.5 Reshapes Coding Economics

Cursor released Composer 2.5, scoring 62 on the Coding Agent Index—a 14-point gain over Composer 2. This puts it in third place behind only Claude Opus 4.7 in Claude Code (66) and GPT-5.5 in Codex (65), while costing roughly 10x less than these alternatives.

Built on Moonshot AI's Kimi K2.5 with additional reinforcement learning, synthetic training, and behavioral improvements, Composer 2.5 offers industry-disruptive pricing: $0.5 per million input tokens and $2.5 per million output tokens for the standard version, with a fast variant at $3.0/$15.0 respectively. The emerging pattern: teams use Composer 2.5 as the default for everyday coding inside Cursor, reaching for Opus 4.7 only for complex architectural reasoning.

Google I/O 2026 Highlights

Google launched Gemini 3.5 Flash as the first model in the new Gemini 3.5 family, already powering Google Search AI Mode and AI Overviews. The company also unveiled Gemini Omni, combining reasoning with content creation tools, allowing users to generate and edit videos using text, images, audio and video prompts through the Gemini app and YouTube Shorts.

Google Cloud CEO Thomas Kurian confirmed that Gemini will power a more personalized Siri launching later in 2026, with Apple paying approximately $1 billion annually for a custom 1.2 trillion parameter Gemini model. This Apple-Google AI partnership represents the most consequential deal in consumer technology since Apple made Google the default search engine, bringing Gemini to two billion Apple devices.

Enterprise AI Adoption Accelerates

Anthropic and PwC announced an expanded strategic alliance, with PwC rolling out Claude Code and Cowork starting with U.S. teams and establishing a joint Center of Excellence to train 30,000 professionals. SAP and Anthropic revealed plans to embed Claude as a primary reasoning capability across SAP's AI-enabled solution portfolio, powered by Joule agents.

NextEra Energy announced a $67 billion acquisition of Dominion Energy—the largest utility merger in US history—with explicit acknowledgment that AI-driven power demand is the primary rationale, signaling America's power infrastructure is being rebuilt around AI data center requirements.

The Bigger Picture

This week's developments reveal a maturing AI ecosystem where differentiation comes from business model choices, not just model capabilities. Anthropic's ad-free commitment and Cursor's cost-efficiency breakthrough suggest that sustainable AI products will win through alignment with user interests, not just raw performance. For aspiring PMs, the lesson is clear: as AI capabilities commoditize, your competitive moat lies in how you structure incentives, price access, and integrate AI into workflows that users actually want to adopt. The companies getting this right are those thinking beyond "better models" to "better products."
