Anthropic Product News

Major Leadership Addition: Andrej Karpathy Joins Pre-Training Team

Andrej Karpathy, the AI researcher who co-founded and formerly worked at OpenAI and previously led AI at Tesla, has joined Anthropic. Karpathy started this week at Anthropic, where he is working on pre-training under team lead Nick Joseph. Pre-training is responsible for the large-scale training runs that give Claude its core knowledge and capabilities. He'll be building a team focused on using Claude to accelerate pretraining research itself. An Anthropic spokesperson confirmed to VentureBeat via email that Karpathy will be starting a team focused on using Claude, Anthropic's own, increasingly popular AI model, to accelerate pretraining research.

Claude Security Enters Public Beta

Anthropic expands Project Glasswing with Claude Security in public beta and new cyber verification tools for eligible security teams, helping scan codebases, triage vulnerabilities, and generate fixes while sharing research and support for open-source defenders. The timing coincides with concerns about Claude Mythos, a model the company says can find software vulnerabilities at a level beyond many human experts.

Enhanced Claude Managed Agents

Anthropic is adding two additional new features for May: MCP tunnels and self-hosted sandboxes. Both the sandbox where an agent executes tools and the services it reaches run within the established boundaries of your enterprise, under your security and runtime controls. The new MCP tunnels feature allows Claude Managed Agents users to route services through a private network. With MCP tunnels, your agents reach MCP servers inside your private network without exposing them to the public internet.

Legal Industry Momentum

Claude releases 20+ new legal MCP connectors and 12 practice-area plugins, expanding how law firms and in-house teams work across research, contracts, discovery, matter management, and legal aid. The update brings deeper workflow support, open customization, and broader access to connected legal tools. Earlier this year we released our first legal plugin, and in the months since, legal professionals have become the most engaged Claude Cowork users of any knowledge-work function.

Feature Deep-Dive

Self-Hosted Sandboxes: Enterprise Control Meets AI Power

Self-hosted sandboxesIsolated environments where code runs safely without affecting main systems represent a major step forward for enterprise Claude deployments. Here's what you need to know:

How it works: With self-hosted sandboxes, you keep sensitive files, packages, and services in your own infrastructure or with a managed sandbox provider. The agent loop that handles orchestration, context management, and error recovery stays on Anthropic's infrastructure, while tool execution moves to your own configured environment.

Security benefits: Inside your perimeter, network policies, audit logging, and security tooling are already in place, and files and repositories don't leave. This addresses enterprise concerns about data sovereignty while maintaining Claude's capabilities.

Performance advantages: You also control the compute: resource sizing and the runtime image are set on your side, so agents running compute-heavy work such as long builds or image generation get the CPU, memory, and capacity the task needs.

Getting started: The feature is in public beta. Bring any sandbox client you want, or start with one of our supported providers: Cloudflare runs sandboxes at scale using microVMs and lighter weight isolates. Explore our docs to learn more, follow our cookbooks to set up your sandbox provider, or deploy your first agent in the Claude Console.

Broader Tech News

Google I/O 2026 Showcases Gemini Ecosystem

Google used I/O 2026 to unveil a broad wave of Gemini-powered products across Search, Android, Workspace, YouTube, shopping, and AI hardware. The company introduced Gemini 3.5 Flash, Gemini Omni, Gemini Spark, AI coding tools, AI-generated interfaces, and new partnerships for Android XR glasses. The message was clear: Google is no longer treating AI as a layer on top of its products. It is rebuilding the product stack around Gemini.

Corporate AI Officer Roles Surge

The report says 76% of the more than 2,000 organizations surveyed have established a new executive office — that of the chief AI officer (CAIO) — up from 26% in 2025. This represents one of the fastest adoptions of a new C-suite role in corporate history, driven by what may be the largest organizational shift since the industrial and digital revolutions.

Meta Workforce Restructuring for AI

Meta initiated the first round of its planned 10% workforce reduction today, cutting roughly 8,000 roles as part of an efficiency drive tied to heavy AI spending. The company had already reassigned about 7,000 employees to new AI-focused groups in recent days, reorganizing teams to prioritize model development and infrastructure.

Global AI Adoption Continues Rising

The global adoption of artificial intelligence continued to rise in the first quarter of 2026. During the quarter, AI usage increased by 1.5 percentage points from 16.3% to 17.8% of the world's working age population, with the UAE continuing to lead global AI diffusion at 70.1%.

The Bigger Picture

Karpathy's move to Anthropic signals more than just another high-profile hire—it represents a fundamental shift in how AI companies think about research and development. By focusing on using Claude to improve Claude's own training processes, Anthropic is betting that recursive self-improvementAI systems that can improve their own training and development processes will be the next competitive frontier. For aspiring product managers, this illustrates a key principle: the most sustainable competitive advantages often come from building better development processes, not just better products. As AI capabilities rapidly advance, the companies that can most efficiently iterate and improve will likely emerge as long-term winners.
